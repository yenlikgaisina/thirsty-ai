Methodology (open)

Each row is a publicly reported UK data centre scheme (announced, submitted, approved or under construction as of June 2026 — not yet operational), compiled from industry trackers, the House of Commons Library, government announcements, regional and local press, and campaign-group coverage. For each we record location, capacity in MW where disclosed, developer, planning status, the water company serving the area, and a source URL. Water-company areas determined to be in serious water stress by the Environment Agency's 2021 classification are flagged. Per-site daily water use is estimated from the government's 2025 "Water use in AI and Data Centres" report using broad per-MW ranges; because cooling technology is rarely disclosed, every figure is a range with wide uncertainty.

Important caveats

The UK has no central data centre planning register, so this is a best-effort compilation, not an exhaustive one. Capacity figures are frequently undisclosed or conflict between sources (flagged in the data). Some "proposed" schemes will never be built, some are paused (e.g. Stargate/Cobalt Park), and some consents are under legal challenge (Iver, Iver Heath) — planning status is labelled per row. Water-company boundaries don't map perfectly to scheme locations; rows with boundary uncertainty are flagged. Scotland and Wales sit outside the EA's England-only water-stress classification and are shown as "not classified", which does not mean water-abundant. Treat all water estimates as indicative ranges only.
